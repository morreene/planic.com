Ideas for life from this inspiring Medium article: [Five Steps to Changing Your Life (they boil down to one thing)](https://medium.com/@shauntagrimes/how-i-changed-my-life-and-you-can-too-d6a0b352a2cd)

1. **Evaluate where you are.** Be honest. Now’s the time to really look at every aspect of your life and notice what’s working and what’s not.
2. **Let your imagination free.** Really think about where you want to be in five years. I love the five year timetable. It’s far enough away to let you really make huge changes — and to have some space for forgiveness if you stumble. And you will. I did. We’re only human.
3. **Make some big, hairy goals.** I mean, big. Don’t worry right now about being reasonable or about managing your expectations. It’s okay to dream. There is value in dreaming big, even if you don’t end up exactly where you thought you would. But, try to make your goals something you have control over. For instance, a goal of writing a book and submitting it to agents (which is only up to you) is better than a goal of being a bestseller in five years (which depends on so many people who aren’t you.)
4. **Think about the next step for each goal.** Just literally, the very next baby step. ... Don’t worry about the step after that (the next many, many steps will look the same anyway — write for ten minutes.) Do that, for every goal.
5. **Be brave.** Change is hard. It’s scary. Human beings are hardwired to seek out the status quo. You don’t have to be brave enough for the end goal though. Not today. You only need to be brave enough for the very next little step.

**Those steps all boil down to this one thing: Keep doing the next thing.**
